About Cambodia

Learn about this fascinating Southeast Asian nation

A Land of Ancient Wonders

Cambodia, officially known as the Kingdom of Cambodia, is a Southeast Asian nation bordered by Thailand to the northwest, Laos to the northeast, Vietnam to the east, and the Gulf of Thailand to the southwest.

With a history dating back to the 1st century AD, Cambodia is home to the magnificent Angkor Wat, the largest religious monument in the world and a symbol of national pride. The country has overcome significant challenges in recent decades to emerge as a peaceful and developing nation.

Today, Cambodia offers visitors a unique blend of ancient temples, pristine beaches, lush jungles, and warm hospitality. The capital city of Phnom Penh is a bustling metropolis where traditional and modern influences coexist.

Rich Cultural Heritage

Experience the traditions and customs of the Khmer people

Traditional Arts

Cambodian classical dance, also known as Apsara dance, is an elegant and graceful art form that dates back to the Angkorian era. These intricate performances depict mythological stories and are an important part of Khmer cultural identity.

Khmer Cuisine

Cambodian food is a delicious blend of flavors influenced by neighboring countries but with its own unique character. Dishes like Fish Amok, Beef Lok Lak, and Nom Banh Chok (noodles) are must-try specialties that showcase the country's culinary heritage.

Festivals & Celebrations

Cambodia celebrates numerous festivals throughout the year, with Khmer New Year (Choul Chnam Thmey) in April being the most important. Other significant celebrations include the Water Festival, Pchum Ben (Ancestor's Day), and the Royal Ploughing Ceremony.

Tourism in Cambodia

Discover the country's most popular destinations and attractions

Explore Cambodia's Treasures

From the ancient temples of Angkor to the pristine beaches of Sihanoukville, Cambodia offers diverse experiences for every traveler. The country's tourism industry has grown significantly in recent years, with improved infrastructure and a wide range of accommodations to suit all budgets.

Whether you're interested in history, culture, nature, or relaxation, Cambodia has something to offer. The warm hospitality of the Khmer people will make your visit truly memorable.

Best time to visit: November to March (cool, dry season)

Angkor Archaeological Park

A UNESCO World Heritage site featuring over 1,000 temples, including the iconic Angkor Wat. A must-visit destination for history and architecture enthusiasts.

Phnom Penh

The vibrant capital city offers a mix of historical sites like the Royal Palace and Silver Pagoda, along with modern markets, restaurants, and a bustling riverside promenade.

Sihanoukville & Islands

Cambodia's premier beach destination with beautiful sandy shores, clear waters, and nearby islands like Koh Rong and Koh Rong Samloem perfect for relaxation and water activities.

Cambodia's Rich History

Journey through the fascinating timeline of the Kingdom

Funan Kingdom (1st-6th century)

The earliest known kingdom in Cambodia, heavily influenced by Indian culture and trading with China and other Southeast Asian regions.

Chenla Kingdom (6th-8th century)

Successor to Funan, Chenla expanded its territory and began the construction of early temple structures that would later evolve into the Angkorian style.

Angkor Empire (9th-15th century)

The golden age of Cambodia, marked by the construction of magnificent temples including Angkor Wat. At its peak, the Khmer Empire was the most powerful in Southeast Asia.

Post-Angkor Period (15th-19th century)

After the decline of Angkor, Cambodia experienced periods of weakness and became a vassal state to Thailand and Vietnam. The capital moved to Phnom Penh in the 15th century.

French Colonial Period (1863-1953)

Cambodia became a French protectorate, which brought infrastructure development but also economic exploitation. Independence was gained in 1953 under King Norodom Sihanouk.

Modern Cambodia (1953-present)

After independence, Cambodia went through periods of peace, war (including the Khmer Rouge regime), and rebuilding. Today, it is a constitutional monarchy with a growing economy.
